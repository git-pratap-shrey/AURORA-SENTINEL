## 🎤 What to Say When Presenting References

**Don't just read the links!** Say this:

> "Our solution is built on proven, peer-reviewed technology:
> 
> **First**, we use WHO data showing 1.6 million annual deaths from violence - this validates the problem we're solving.
> 
> **Second**, our ML engine uses YOLOv8, the industry-standard object detection model trusted by thousands of companies worldwide.
> 
> **Third**, our AI layer leverages Qwen2-VL, a cutting-edge vision-language model published in top-tier AI conferences.
> 
> We're not reinventing the wheel - we're standing on the shoulders of giants and applying proven technology to save lives."

---

## 💡 Pro Tips for References

1. **Keep it to 3-5 references max** - More looks desperate
2. **Use authoritative sources** - WHO, IEEE, ArXiv, Google
3. **Mix types** - Statistics + Technology + Research
4. **Make them scannable** - QR codes are impressive
5. **Don't spend too much time** - 10-15 seconds max on this slide
